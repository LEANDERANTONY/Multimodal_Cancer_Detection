# ADR-001 - Scope and Architecture Updates for Thesis Execution
**Date:** 2025-08-08  
**Status:** Accepted  
**Author:** Leander Antony A  

---

## Context

The original research proposal outlined a multimodal deep learning pipeline for early pancreatic cancer detection using:

- paired CT imaging
- urinary biomarkers
- blood biomarkers

The initial plan assumed a cleaner multimodal setting than the one available in practice. During implementation planning and execution, several constraints became unavoidable:

1. **Data availability**
   - public CT and biomarker datasets were available, but they were not patient-paired
   - no public dataset provided CT plus urinary plus blood biomarkers for the same patients

2. **Compute constraints**
   - local hardware was not suitable for the heaviest CT deep learning runs
   - Google Colab Pro became the practical execution environment for CT training

3. **Methodological constraints**
   - cross-dataset CT bias emerged as a central issue
   - synthetic pairing could support fusion methodology experiments, but not clinically valid multimodal claims

---

## Decision

The following scope and architecture changes were made.

### 1. Drop blood biomarker modelling from the maintained thesis scope

- **Original plan:** use both urinary and blood biomarkers
- **Decision:** focus on urinary biomarkers only
- **Why:**
  - no suitable public multimodal cohort supported the broader biochemical scope
  - this avoided compounding the synthetic pairing problem
  - it kept the experimental scope feasible

### 2. Use a unimodal-first strategy with exploratory fusion

- **Original plan:** build a true multimodal fusion model on paired data
- **Decision:**
  - build and evaluate CT and urinary biomarker models independently on real data
  - run fusion experiments only under explicitly synthetic class-matched pairing
- **Why:**
  - this preserved the multimodal research question without overstating the evidence
  - it enabled controlled fusion comparisons under the real data constraints
  - it kept the study scientifically defensible

### 3. Center the CT pipeline on bias-aware preprocessing rather than an ROI-detector-first design

- **Original plan:** direct CNN classification with stronger ROI-oriented ideas considered during planning
- **Decision:** implement the CT workflow around:
  - DICOM-to-PNG conversion with soft-tissue windowing
  - explicit bias detection
  - body-region-specific extreme intensity standardization
  - orientation correction
  - body segmentation and cropping
  - ResNet50-based CT classification on the resulting cropped images
- **Why:**
  - the practical bottleneck turned out to be cross-dataset shortcut learning, not just pancreas localization
  - the implemented dissertation pipeline relied on bias-aware preprocessing plus cropping, not YOLO-based ROI detection
  - this better reflected the actual system that was trained and evaluated

### 4. Compare fusion strategies under strict caveats

- **Original plan:** use a generic multimodal fusion block
- **Decision:** evaluate decision-level and feature-level fusion under synthetic pairing, with negative controls and multi-seed checks
- **Why:**
  - this made it possible to ask whether fusion behaved meaningfully under constraints
  - it emphasized methodological rigor over optimistic multimodal claims

### 5. Split compute strategy across environments

- **Original plan:** train all models locally
- **Decision:**
  - CT-heavy experiments run in Google Colab Pro
  - biomarker and lighter analysis work run locally
- **Why:**
  - CT training needed GPU support
  - tabular biomarker modelling and report-generation tasks were lightweight enough for local execution

---

## Consequences

**Positive:**

- kept the thesis feasible under time, compute, and dataset constraints
- foregrounded bias-aware methodology instead of hiding cross-dataset risk behind strong metrics
- preserved a usable foundation for future matched-cohort multimodal work
- allowed transparent reporting of negative or ambiguous findings

**Negative:**

- no clinically valid paired multimodal performance claim can be made
- blood biomarkers are outside the maintained scope
- CT performance remains scientifically ambiguous because residual domain structure may still influence deep features
- fusion results are exploratory and structurally limited by synthetic pairing

---

## Post-Thesis Interpretation

The final dissertation results sharpen the meaning of this ADR:

- the urinary biomarker branch became the clearest positive result and strongest reproducibility story
- the CT branch remained promising but scientifically ambiguous because residual domain structure persisted beyond pixel-level mitigation
- the fusion branch was most valuable as a controlled negative result, demonstrating modality dominance under synthetic pairing rather than genuine multimodal synergy

---

## Future Work

When stronger data becomes available, the next steps are:

1. validate the CT branch on additional external institutions
2. replace synthetic pairing with real patient-level matched CT and biomarker cohorts
3. re-evaluate fusion under those matched conditions
4. expand biochemical scope only when the data support it responsibly
